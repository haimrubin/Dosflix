Motivation & Problem Definition

The Challenge

  • Mainstream platforms contain inappropriate content
  • Fragmented Torah content across multiple platforms
  • Exposure to secular distractions and advertisements
  • Rabbis hesitant to share content on general platforms
  • Lack of community-driven religious content platform

The Solution

  • 100% kosher, curated content
  • Safe environment for spiritual growth
  • User-generated content with admin approval
  • Modern streaming experience with religious values
  • Community engagement without compromise

Literature Review

Cultural Sensitivity in UI/UX Design

Research emphasizes the importance of incorporating cultural contexts into digital platforms, particularly for religious audiences where symbols, colors, and imagery carry profound significance.

Religious Digital Platform Design

Contemporary research underscores that designers must balance technological innovation with cultural sensitivity, spiritual appropriateness, and diverse user needs when creating platforms for religious communities.

User Experience for Sacred Content

Studies show that robust technical infrastructure combined with intuitive design principles can create platforms that enhance user satisfaction and engagement while maintaining respect for the sacred nature of religious content.

Study Methodology

Comprehensive Google Forms survey distributed to 22 volunteers (20 men, 2 women) with mean age of 14 years from the religious Jewish community over a two-week testing period.

User Study Results Analysis

Usage Patterns

Daily Usage: 57.9% expect daily use

Content Discovery:

  • Homepage: 73.7%
  • Search by topic: 47.4%
  • Search by lecturer: 26.3%

Feature Adoption:

  • Favorites: 63.2% active users
  • Search: 78.9% regular usage
